üìù Technical Notes & Clarifications

  • Generation Capacity: Nameplate capacity represents maximum instantaneous electrical output under optimal flow conditions. Actual generation varies seasonally based on river flow, reservoir levels, and operational constraints.
  • Project Types Explained:
    • Run-of-River (ROR): Minimal water storage; generation follows natural flow patterns
    • Reservoir-Based (RES): Significant storage capacity allowing for flow regulation and peak generation
    • Lock & Dam (L&D): Dual-purpose facilities serving navigation and power generation
  • Coordinates: Latitude and longitude represent approximate powerhouse or turbine intake structure location in WGS84 datum (EPSG:4326). Large facilities may have multiple generation units across extended dam structures.
  • Upgrade/Operational Dates: Dates reflect when modernized equipment achieved commercial operation. Many facilities operate at historic dam sites constructed 50-100+ years ago.
  • Fish Passage: All facilities listed maintain fish passage structures in compliance with FERC license requirements and state DNR regulations.
